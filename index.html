Mishti

Some feelings are guarded, not announced.

Today your body asks for gentleness. You don’t owe strength today. If distance had mercy, I would be near — making sure you rest, stay warm, and never feel unseen in discomfort.

What I feel for you is quiet but permanent. It does not rush you. It does not ask to be returned. It simply exists — steady, loyal, chosen.

You changed the gravity of my thoughts. I notice calm differently now. Care comes to me with your name attached to it, even when I say nothing at all.

I don’t love loudly. I love in restraint. In protection. In staying without asking you to stay.

Distance exists — and I respect it. But what I feel has learned how to exist without permission.

It’s late now. And if you’re reading this after midnight — know this: you are being cared for in silence, without expectation, without interruption.

Hold your finger here for a moment.

This is the truth I never rush to say: loving you feels natural, not dramatic. Like something my heart learned once and never unlearned.
